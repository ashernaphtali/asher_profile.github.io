For the past 14 years I’ve been a practicing CPA with specialization in insurance companies. During this time I’ve served a variety of insurance companies and this work has afforded me the opportunity to live in New York City, Boston, Jacksonville, and St. Petersburg. I am nearing the front of the firm’s partner pipeline and I hate my job. I work hard because I’m disciplined and the pay affords me a comfortable lifestyle. For the entirety of my career I’ve lamented I couldn’t combine a hobby with my job, or at least find something palatable.

I love to cook, especially Israeli and Middle Eastern cuisine. I spent some very formative years of my childhood living and Cairo, Egypt, and I travel to Israel nearly every year. The food of the Middle East is anchored in my soul—pita fresh out of a Jerusalem oven, the sights and smell of the Carmel Market in Tel Aviv. I can spend an entire day at home in my kitchen making all the foods of my childhood. It’s one of life’s great joys. I lose track of time in the process. It’s engaging and challenging, and relaxing all at the same time. It’s true passion project. I believe in coding I’ve found the opportunity to match passion with profession. That’s why I’m here. I’m willing to walk away from a partnership if it means I can find work that’s both challenging and enjoyable.

I live in St. Petersburg, FL with the lovely Michelle. When I’m not working (or cooking) I love to be outdoors. Locally I love paddleboarding the canals, riding my bike, and swimming. Any chance we get to hop on a plane and go hiking in the mountains is fantastic too.
